Fr En Ar

Applied R&D on management of genetic resources


During my first 15 years of career, I was actively involved in the field of R&D in animal sciences and management of genetic resources. As head Tunisian National animal database and R&D (OEP), I accomplished the tasks of i) Implementation and management of the animal and health tracebility database of the Tunisian national program of genetic improvement (CAG-Sidi Thabet, OEP), ii) animal performance data valuation and development of decision-making, herd management and animal selection.


In applied R&D, I had a very active collaboration in breeding, production chains and the management of genetic resources with Tunisian research institutions (ESAM, INAT, ENMVT), German, Austria, France, Italy, Belgium, and Luxembourg technical cooperation projects. I had an fructufill collobaration with FAO regional office for the Near East and Africa in field of small ruminant breeding.
This experience allowed me to deepen my knowledge in genetics and breeding improvement under various conditions and diversified breeding systems ranging from above ground to intensive. Aware of the importance of data in breeding, I was strongly involved in the digitilisation of its collection, analysis and valuation for risk management and timely decision-making.

Basic & Fondamental research


My integration as a scientific researcher for 16 years in Belgium allowed me to gain profond skills in the field of animal production sciences with expertise in statistics, data modeling, numerical compuing, genetics, networking and management research projects.


My FNRS postdoctoral felow research project addressed current bottlenecks related to non-availability of direct and complex measures of adaption and mitigation to handle novel challenges and unravel the system complexity under anticipated climate change scenarios. Research activities were oriented towards aspects of genetic diversity, resilience, efficiency and transition on an animal scale and also breeding systems as improving elements of the animal's capacity to emerging diseases, nutritional, reproductive and environmental challenges.


Achievements in the field of heat stress studies were attracting for several other research groups and enhanced an active collaboration with researchers in animal adapation to climate change (MACSUR project, FACCE-JPI), in the transition to organic and low-input systems (2-Org-Cows, CORE organic), in animal welfare (DairyCare, EU Cost Project) and other international networks dealing with genetic adaptation and mitigation under climate change (Global Research Alliance “GRA” and Animal Selection Genetics and Genomics Network “ASGGN”).

Senior Scientific Researcher


To accelerate the progress on efficiency, resilience and sustainability, my interaction with breeding sector, relevant stackeholders under several international projects with a large consortia of researchers and milk recording and selection organisations allowed me to improve my knowledge and my research activities in the field of the development of improved tools to accelerate the identification and exploitation of phenotypic, genetic and genomic characteristics for complex, rare and difficult to measure routinely traits.


I have contributed significantly to several multi-actor European research projects (e.g. OptiMIR (INTERREG-NWE IVb) and GplusE (FP7) and also local multi-partner in Walloon region of Belgium (e.g. MIRHeatStress (SPW-DGO7)). These projects were focused of the exploitation of milk-based biomarkers and the capitalization of MIR spectra, high throughput data acquisition at phenotypic and genomic levels on animals, environments, and genes for genetic selection and herd management.


I acquired a good capacity to produce scientific results of high quality (around 30 peer reviewed publications are in journals in the first quartile in the Agriculture, dairy and animal science category), to disseminate results in national and international meetings (40) and to interact with the dairy industry to facilitate technology transfer.
